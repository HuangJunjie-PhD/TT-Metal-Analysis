# Power and Thermal Management

Relevant source files
*   [README.md](https://github.com/tenstorrent/tt-firmware/blob/c9a325bb/README.md?plain=1)

## Purpose and Scope

This document describes the power and thermal management subsystem within the tt-firmware. This critical component ensures Tenstorrent hardware operates within safe power and temperature limits, protecting the device while enabling optimal performance. The power and thermal management system interfaces with hardware sensors and control mechanisms to monitor and regulate power consumption and heat generation across all supported board types.

For information about the System Management Processor that coordinates these functions, see [System Management Processor](https://deepwiki.com/tenstorrent/tt-firmware/2.1-system-management-processor). For details on I/O connectivity that may impact power states, see [I/O Connectivity](https://deepwiki.com/tenstorrent/tt-firmware/2.2-io-connectivity).

## System Overview

The Power and Thermal Management subsystem is a core responsibility of the System Management Processor. It continuously monitors power consumption and temperature readings from various sensors, applying appropriate control actions to maintain operation within safe limits. This includes power state transitions, thermal throttling, and emergency shutdown procedures when necessary.

## Power Management

The power management component is responsible for ensuring that Tenstorrent devices operate within their specified power envelopes. This involves monitoring power consumption, regulating voltage levels, and managing different power states.

### Power Monitoring and Regulation

The firmware continuously samples power-related sensors to track:

*   Current power consumption
*   Voltage levels across critical components
*   Current draw from various subsystems

Based on these readings, the firmware applies appropriate control actions to maintain operation within safe and efficient parameters.

**Figure 2: Power Monitoring and Control Flow**

Sources: [README.md 8-13](https://github.com/tenstorrent/tt-firmware/blob/c9a325bb/README.md?plain=1#L8-L13)

### Power States

The firmware manages several power states to balance performance, power consumption, and thermal output:

| Power State | Description | Typical Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Full Performance | Maximum power allowed for peak performance | Heavy computation workloads |
| Balanced | Moderate power limits for sustained operation | Normal operation |
| Low Power | Reduced performance to minimize power consumption | Idle or power-constrained environments |
| Sleep | Minimal power consumption with rapid wake capability | Temporary inactivity |
| Shutdown | Powering off components safely | System termination |

## Thermal Management

Thermal management is essential for preventing overheating damage and maintaining stable operation. The firmware uses temperature readings from multiple sensors to apply appropriate cooling or throttling mechanisms.

### Temperature Monitoring

Temperature sensors are distributed throughout the device to provide granular thermal information:

*   Core temperature sensors
*   Memory temperature sensors
*   Board-level temperature sensors
*   Ambient temperature sensors

**Figure 3: Thermal Monitoring and Response System**

Sources: [README.md 8-13](https://github.com/tenstorrent/tt-firmware/blob/c9a325bb/README.md?plain=1#L8-L13)

### Thermal Protection Mechanisms

The firmware implements a tiered approach to thermal protection:

1.   **Passive Cooling**: Increasing fan speeds (where available) to improve heat dissipation
2.   **Thermal Throttling**: Reducing performance to decrease heat generation
3.   **Emergency Shutdown**: Powering down the device to prevent damage when critical temperature thresholds are exceeded

## Board-Specific Implementations

Each Tenstorrent board type has specific power and thermal management characteristics based on its hardware design and operational requirements.

### Grayskull

The Grayskull implementation focuses on maintaining stability in datacenter environments with external cooling systems. It monitors multiple temperature sensors and can trigger appropriate throttling when needed.

### Wormhole

Wormhole builds upon the Grayskull implementation with enhanced thermal management capabilities to handle higher power densities and potentially more complex cooling arrangements.

### Blackhole

Blackhole features the most advanced power and thermal management capabilities of the current board lineup. It includes more granular control over power states and improved thermal response mechanisms to handle its higher performance envelope.

### Critical Interactions

The power and thermal management subsystem has several important interactions with other system components:

1.   **Hardware Initialization**: During startup, power sequencing ensures components receive power in the correct order
2.   **I/O Connectivity**: Power states can affect PCIe, Ethernet, and memory subsystems
3.   **Performance Management**: Thermal conditions may require dynamically adjusting performance parameters

## Operational Parameters

The firmware maintains device operation within specific power and thermal parameters:

| Parameter | Purpose | Management Method |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Power Envelope | Ensure device operates within power supply capabilities | Dynamic voltage and frequency scaling |
| Junction Temperature | Prevent silicon damage from overheating | Thermal throttling and emergency shutdown |
| Board Temperature | Prevent damage to board components | Fan control and throttling |
| Ambient Temperature | Ensure proper operation within environmental limits | Adaptive thermal management |
